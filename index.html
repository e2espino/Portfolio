About Me

Recent UC San Diego graduate with a B.S. in General Biology with a specialization in Chemistry. 2+ year of experience as a medical scribe, Spanish interpreter, and department coordinator at Palomar Health. Possesses specialized knowledge in medical terminology and EHR systems, as well as human anatomy and the biological basis for disease. Passionate on advocating for affordable and accessible healthcare for all. Dedicated the last 2 years developing a proficiency in large-scale project management for various non-profit organizations fostered at UCSD, including Circle K and Flying Samaritans. Passionate on providing free medical and humanitarian services to the underserved populations across San Diego and Ensenada, Mexico. Pursuing a career in medicine, both as a biomedical researcher and an endocrinologist. As an undergrad, received specialized training in molecular biology techniques as a research intern with the Fricovsky Lab, which is fostered at the UCSD Skaggs School of Pharmacy. Alongside my pursuit of higher education in medicine, is a life-long mission to create accessible systems to establish innovative systems to provide humanitarian resources in highly-impacted populations, promote health and science education for all, while expanding the parameters of biomedical research.
